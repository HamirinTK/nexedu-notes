## 1. What is a Variable?

In mathematics, a **variable** is a symbol (usually a letter like $x$, $y$, or $z$) that represents an unknown or changeable quantity. Think of it as a blank box where you can put different numbers.

### Real-World Example
Imagine you are buying apples. If each apple costs 2 dollars, the total cost depends on how many apples you buy. 
* If you buy $a$ apples, the total cost is $2 \times a$ (or simply $2a$).
* In this scenario, $a$ is our variable.

## 2. Constants vs. Variables

It is important to distinguish between things that change and things that stay the same:
* **Constant:** A fixed value, like `7`, `100`, or `3.14`. It never changes.
* **Variable:** A placeholder that can take on different values, like `x` or `n`.

> **Note:** Whenever you see a number attached directly to a variable, that number is called a *coefficient*. It means you multiply the variable by that number. For example:

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$$
